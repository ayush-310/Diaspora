Marriage ceremony in Hinduism.

Hindu wedding symbolize spiritual union, family continuity, and the fulfillment of life’s goals. Marriage is regarded to be a sacrament by Hindus, rather than a form of social contract.

Marriage ceremony in Islam.

Marriage in Islam is more than just a social contract—it’s a sacred covenant that binds a man and a woman in a relationship grounded in mutual rights, responsibilities, and respect.

Marriage ceremony in Sikhism.

Sikh marriages celebrate love, equality, and spiritual union. They are devoid of elaborate rituals and focus on simplicity, humility, and devotion.

Marriage ceremony in Christianity.

Christian marriage is often referred to as a covenant. The word “covenant” comes from Hebrew and means “to cut.” It signifies a solemn and binding agreement between the couple, witnessed by God and their community..

Nāmakarana ceremony in Hinduism

A rite that commemorates the naming of the child, usually one to five weeks after birth. It may be celebrated at the local community’s temple in the presence of a priest.

Ramadan in Islam.

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar, observed by Muslims worldwide as a month of fasting, prayer, reflection, and community.
